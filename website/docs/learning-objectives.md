# Learning Objectives: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Course-Level Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Understand Physical AI Fundamentals**: Explain the core concepts of Physical AI and how they differ from traditional AI systems that operate in digital environments.

2. **Analyze Robot Kinematics**: Apply mathematical models to understand and predict robot movement and interaction with the physical environment.

3. **Design Control Systems**: Develop control algorithms for robotic systems that can adapt to dynamic environments and maintain stability.

4. **Implement AI for Humanoid Systems**: Apply advanced AI techniques to create humanoid robots capable of complex behaviors and interactions.

5. **Evaluate Human-Robot Interaction**: Critically assess the effectiveness of AI-driven humanoid systems in real-world applications.

## Module-Level Learning Objectives

### Module 1: Foundations of Physical AI
- Define Physical AI and explain its applications
- Compare Physical AI with traditional AI approaches
- Understand sensorimotor learning principles
- Analyze embodied cognition concepts

### Module 2: Robot Kinematics and Dynamics
- Apply forward and inverse kinematics calculations
- Model robot dynamics for motion planning
- Understand degrees of freedom in robotic systems
- Analyze static and dynamic stability

### Module 3: Control Systems and Locomotion
- Design feedback control systems for robots
- Implement walking and balancing algorithms
- Understand gait generation and adaptation
- Analyze energy efficiency in locomotion

### Module 4: Advanced AI for Humanoid Systems
- Implement machine learning for humanoid control
- Design perception systems for humanoid robots
- Integrate multimodal sensing and actuation
- Evaluate humanoid behavior in real-world scenarios

## Assessment Methods

Students will demonstrate achievement of these learning objectives through:
- Practical assignments and simulations
- Midterm and final examinations
- Hands-on robotics projects
- Peer evaluations and presentations

## Prerequisites Verification

Before beginning this course, students should verify they can:
- Perform basic matrix operations and vector calculations
- Write Python programs using libraries like NumPy
- Understand fundamental physics concepts (forces, motion, energy)
