# Module 3, Week 10: Energy Efficiency in Locomotion

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
- Analyze power consumption in robotic locomotion
- Implement optimal control for efficiency
- Utilize passive dynamics in locomotion
- Apply spring-loaded inverted pendulum models
- Compare different locomotion strategies for efficiency

## Topics Covered

### Day 1: Power Consumption Analysis
- Energy measurement in robotics
- Power consumption factors
- Efficiency metrics
- Battery life considerations

### Day 2: Optimal Control for Efficiency
- Energy-optimal control formulations
- Pontryagin's minimum principle
- Dynamic optimization
- Control efficiency vs. stability trade-offs

### Day 3: Passive Dynamics Utilization
- Passive dynamic walking
- Exploiting natural dynamics
- Underactuated systems
- Energy-efficient mechanisms

### Day 4: Spring-Loaded Inverted Pendulum Models
- SLIP model applications
- Leg compliance and efficiency
- Energy storage and release
- Spring optimization

### Day 5: Comparison of Locomotion Strategies
- Walking vs. running efficiency
- Different gait comparisons
- Terrain-specific efficiency
- Human vs. robot efficiency
