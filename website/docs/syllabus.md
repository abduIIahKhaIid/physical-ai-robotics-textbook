# Course Syllabus: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Course Schedule: 13-Week Program

This comprehensive course is structured over 13 weeks, with each module spanning multiple weeks to allow for deep understanding and practical application.

### Week 1-3: Module 1 - Foundations of Physical AI
- **Week 1**: Introduction to Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence
- **Week 2**: Sensorimotor Learning and Perception
- **Week 3**: Embodied Cognition and Environmental Interaction
- **Module 1 Assessment**: Quiz and Practical Exercise

### Week 4-6: Module 2 - Robot Kinematics and Dynamics
- **Week 4**: Forward and Inverse Kinematics
- **Week 5**: Robot Dynamics and Motion Planning
- **Week 6**: Degrees of Freedom and Stability Analysis
- **Module 2 Assessment**: Simulation Project

### Week 7-10: Module 3 - Control Systems and Locomotion
- **Week 7**: Feedback Control Systems
- **Week 8**: Walking Algorithms and Balance Control
- **Week 9**: Gait Generation and Adaptation
- **Week 10**: Energy Efficiency in Locomotion
- **Module 3 Assessment**: Control System Design Project

### Week 11-13: Module 4 - Advanced AI for Humanoid Systems
- **Week 11**: Machine Learning for Humanoid Control
- **Week 12**: Perception Systems and Multimodal Integration
- **Week 13**: Real-World Applications and Evaluation
- **Module 4 Assessment**: Capstone Project

## Weekly Structure

Each week typically includes:
- **Lectures**: 2-3 hours of theoretical concepts
- **Practical Sessions**: 2-4 hours of hands-on exercises
- **Reading Assignments**: 1-2 research papers or textbook chapters
- **Discussion Forums**: Peer interaction and problem-solving
- **Assessment Activities**: Quizzes, coding exercises, or projects

## Learning Activities

### Lectures and Theory
- Interactive lectures covering core concepts
- Case studies from real-world applications
- Guest speakers from industry and academia

### Practical Exercises
- Simulation environments for experimentation
- Programming assignments in Python
- Virtual robot control challenges
- Data analysis and visualization tasks

### Projects and Assessments
- Individual and group projects
- Progressive complexity from basic to advanced
- Peer review and collaborative learning
- Portfolio development for professional growth
